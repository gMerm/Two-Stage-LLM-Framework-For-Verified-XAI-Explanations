### Actionable Takeaways

- **Focus on Alcohol & Sulphates**: These two features carry the largest predictive weight. Ensuring optimal alcohol levels (typically around 12–13 % v/v for high‑quality reds) and maintaining sulphate concentrations in the 0.5–2.0 g/L range can markedly improve quality scores.
- **Monitor Acidic Balance**: Keep volatile acidity low (≤ 0.6 g/L) and manage citric acid levels to keep pH between 3.2–3.6, which supports a higher quality classification.
- **Control Sulfur Levels**: Balance total sulfur dioxide and free sulfur dioxide to avoid spoilage while preserving the wine; both high total SO₂ and moderate free SO₂ contribute positively.
- **Watch for Negative Interactions**: High chlorides, residual sugar, or density can offset positive contributions from other features, so maintain these at levels consistent with high‑quality standards.
