### Why these features matter

- **Alcohol**: The strongest single driver; it reflects the wine’s body and balance, both of which are key quality attributes.  
- **Sulphates**: Act as antioxidants; higher levels help preserve the wine’s character, improving perceived quality.  
- **Sulfur dioxide (total & free)**: Used as a preservative, but excess can mask aromas and reduce quality, hence the negative direction.  
- **Volatile acidity**: Excessive acetic acid imparts a vinegar taste, lowering quality.  
- **Citric acid & pH**: Together they influence the wine’s freshness and stability; the interaction captures how acidity balances with overall acidity.  
- **Density**: Correlates with alcohol content and sugar; lower density typically indicates higher alcohol.  
- **Chlorides**: High salt content can be perceived as harsh, reducing quality.  
- **Residual sugar**: Adds body and sweetness; moderate levels improve quality, but too much can lead to sweetness overpowering balance.  
- **Interactions**: The EBM model shows that some features amplify or mitigate each other’s effects. For instance, the negative impact of high total SO₂ is lessened when sulphates are also high, suggesting a balancing role.
