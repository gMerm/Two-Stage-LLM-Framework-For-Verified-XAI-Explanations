### Why these features matter for wine quality

* **Alcohol** – Higher alcohol content generally indicates a fuller body and more complex flavors, traits associated with higher quality scores.  
* **Sulphates** – Sulphates act as a preservative and enhance aroma stability; moderate levels contribute to perceived quality.  
* **Sulfur dioxide (total and free)** – These compounds protect against oxidation but excessive levels can introduce off‑flavors, lowering perceived quality.  
* **Volatile acidity** – Elevated volatile acidity often signals spoilage or over‑fermentation, a hallmark of low quality.  
* **Citric acid** – Provides a bright, fresh profile; its balance with pH is critical for overall taste.  
* **Density** – Captures the combined effect of alcohol and sugar; denser wines tend to be richer, aligning with higher quality.  
* **Residual sugar** – Determines sweetness; too high or too low sugar levels can detract from quality depending on style.  
* **Chlorides** – Saltiness can accentuate certain flavor notes but excess chloride is undesirable, affecting quality.
