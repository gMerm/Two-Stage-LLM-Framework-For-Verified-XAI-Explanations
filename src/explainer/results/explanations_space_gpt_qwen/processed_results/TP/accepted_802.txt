### Why These Features Matter

- **Alcohol**: Provides body and perceived quality; higher alcohol is generally associated with premium wines.  
- **Sulphates**: Acts as a preservative; moderate levels improve aroma stability without masking flavor.  
- **Sulfur dioxide (total & free)**: Protects against oxidation but excess can cause off‑flavors.  
- **Acidic measures (volatile acidity, citric acid, pH)**: Balance flavor, freshness, and stability.  
- **Density & residual sugar**: Reflect composition and potential sweetness; lower values often signal balanced, dry wines.  
- **Chlorides**: High saltiness can be undesirable; lower levels favor quality.

3. **Total SO₂ & Sulphates** – A balance where both are moderate helps preserve aroma without imparting harshness.  
4. **pH & Sulphates** – Low pH with adequate sulphates supports flavor stability while maintaining brightness.  
5. **Total SO₂ & Density** – Low sulfur and lighter wine weight together enhance mouthfeel and quality perception.
